  • Problems
  • Political Apathy

    More votes were cast for Taylor Hicks, a contestant in the 2006 American Idol contest, than for any president in any election in US history.

  • Health and Wellbeing

    By the age of 45 largely preventable diseases like heart disease, cancer and diabetes cause the vast majority of deaths in both rich and poor nations. Giving up smoking between the ages of 60 and 75 reduces the risk of premature death by 50%.

  • Income Inequality

    The greater the gap between rich and poor in a country, the higher its rates of mental illness, infant mortality, adult obesity, teen pregnancy, homicide and imprisonment.

  • Privacy

    Website history can be traced to your computer's IP address, so data "may be shared" with governments, litigants or divorcing spouses.
    Your search information is logged and stored indefinitely and recent changes to Google's privacy policy lets it merge data between Youtube, Gmail and Google+.

  • Terrorism

    Traffic accidents kill around 2,500 people in the UK every year. Since the 'war on terror' began the largest number of victims in a UK mainland terrorist attack is 52.
    Yet the official threat level has never been less than substantial and we fear terrorists more than cars.

  • Fraud

    Tax fraud costs the UK government at least £15 billion a year, around 15 times as much as benefit fraud.
    Online scams like fraudulent dating profiles increase yearly: that girl may not be quite as perfect as she seems, or even a girl at all.

  • Surveillance Society

    Britain has more surveillance camera monitoring streets, parts and government buildings than any other country.
    It is now routine for employers to monitor staff internet use and keep track of their whereabouts using GPS technology on their mobile phones.

  • Economic Crisis

    The UK spent £38 billion on bailing out the banks, enough to pay for four London Olympics, 70 new hospitals or 1,520 new schools.

  • Self Image

    A survey found that almost a third of women would trade at least a year of their lives to be their ideal body weight and shape.
    Eighty percent wanted to lose weight even though they were within the underweight or normal weight range.

  • Stuff and Consumerism

    Rioters in Britain took to the streets not to protest some policy or support a movement but to gain new sports shoes and flat screen TVs. They were the riots of 'disqualified consumers' (Zygmunt Bauman).
    Today, we are shopper and consumers before we are citizens.

  • Mental Health

    Today one in six workers is suffering from stress, anxiety or depression. Look around your office, if you have half a dozen co-workers is's probably one of them, if it isn't you.

  • Climate Change

    The effects of climate change can already be observed in the UK and around the world. Extreme weather is predicted to become more common with catastrophic effects on human, animal and plant life within our children's lifetimes.

  • The Ageing

    An eighty year old today can expect to live up to 91, but health spans do not necessarily keep up with life spans. As Eubie Blake remarked in his late nineties, "If I'd known I was going to live this long I would have taken better care of myself".
